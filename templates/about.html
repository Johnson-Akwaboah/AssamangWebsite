About Domi Assamang Community

Building unity, development, and a brighter future for all.

Who We Are

Domi Assamang Community is a vibrant and united community dedicated to promoting peace, development, education, and social progress. We are made up of hardworking individuals, families, youth, elders, and leaders who share a common vision of creating a better future for everyone.

Through cooperation and collective effort, we continue to support initiatives that improve the quality of life of our people. Our community values respect, unity, honesty, and service, which guide us in everything we do.

We believe that sustainable development can only be achieved when community members work together toward common goals. Therefore, we actively encourage participation in projects, educational activities, social programs, and community events.

Our History

Domi Assamang has a rich cultural heritage and a long tradition of unity and cooperation. Over the years, the community has grown through the dedication and hard work of its people.

Community leaders, youth groups, women associations, religious bodies, and volunteers have continuously worked together to promote development and improve living standards within the area.

Through fundraising activities, development projects, educational support initiatives, and welfare programs, the community has continued to make meaningful progress while preserving its traditions and values.

History of the Community Logo

The Domi Assamang Community logo was designed by Mr. Okyere Gideon (Ogidy), Director of Perfect Hands Printing Solutions. As a proud citizen of Domi Assamang, he dedicated his creativity and expertise to designing the official community logo.

The logo consists of three important symbols:

  1. Dove with Holy Bible – Symbolizes peace, faith, and Christianity.
  2. Cocoa – Represents farming and agriculture, which remain important sources of livelihood within the community.
  3. Gold – Represents the natural blessings and resources that God has bestowed upon the land.

The logo was approved by the Chiefs, Assembly Members, Unit Committee Chairman, and committee members of the community.

It was officially dedicated by Nana Adu Gyamfi Nyarko II during the community fundraising event held in support of the Community Center project on 23rd April 2025.
